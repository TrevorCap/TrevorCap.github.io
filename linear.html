In order to predict the strikeout and walk rates with machine learning, we can use a tool called the Random Forest Regressor. This tool uses a series of decision trees and selects sub samples of the data to avoid overfitting. When regressing the figures with the Random Forest Regressor, we are able to predict the strikeouts, walks, and combined strikeout minus walk rates for pitchers with limited data.


For expected strikeout rates we are able to use the data from April 2019 and come back with an R2 of 0.46.


For expected walk rates we are able to use the data from April 2019 and come back with an R2 of 0.06, which is Not Great!


For expected strikeout minus walk rates we are able to use the data from April 2019 and come back with an R2 of 0.25.
